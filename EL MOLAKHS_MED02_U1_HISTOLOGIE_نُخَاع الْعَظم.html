    • It consists of 03 main components:
      • Reticular framework
      • Blood vessels
      • Free cells.
    • Reticular Framework:
    • Composed of a fine network of reticular fibers extending from the walls of blood vessels to the bony trabeculae. Within the spaces of this network are mainly reticular cells, fat cells, and macrophages.
    • Reticular Cells:
    • Located about 50% around sinusoidal capillaries, they control cellular exchanges between the hematopoietic tissue and sinusoidal capillaries.
    • These are mesenchymal cells that can differentiate into endothelial cells, fibrous fibers, osteoblasts, and fat cells.
    • They can synthesize extracellular matrix fibers and some growth factors (necessary for blood formation) or accumulate fat and transform into fat cells.
    • Fat Cells:
    • Large round cells with a small nucleus located at the periphery and a large space filling the cytoplasm.
    • Scarce in red marrow and abundant in yellow marrow.
    • Represent 20-40% of the bone marrow.
    • They secrete fatty acids, cholesterol esters, and leptin that stimulate the growth of erythroblastic, granular, and monocytic lineages.
    • Blood Vessels:
    • Represented by discontinuous capillaries called sinusoidal capillaries, lined with loosely connected endothelial cells.
    • Accompanied by fibrous fibers, mast cells, plasma, and Kupffer cells participating in blood purification (such as destroying old red blood cells).
    • Free Cells:
    • Contain hematopoietic stem cells responsible for the long-term renewal of all blood cells, both white and red blood cells.
    • Multipotent stem cells that differentiate into unipotent stem cells (colony-forming units CFU).
    • When a stem cell divides, one daughter cell maintains the stem cell pool, while the other differentiates.
    • Erythroblastic Lineage Cells:
    • Gathered in islands or clusters centered on a macrophage within marrow spaces near the sinuses.
    • Erythroblastic maturation is characterized by a reduction in cell size (nucleus and cytoplasm), decreased basophilia of the cytoplasm, and increased hemoglobin concentration.
    • Development Stages:
      • 1- Proerythroblast: large cell with a clear round nucleus containing 1-3 small nucleoli, basophilic cytoplasm.
      • 2- Basophilic erythroblast: smaller nucleus, denser chromatin, small nucleus.
      • 3- Polychromatic then acidophilic erythroblast: last maturation stage, small round nucleus, dark and homogeneous chromatin, pink or clear cytoplasm.
      • 4- Reticulocyte, red blood cells.

    • Scattered and mature in a centripetal manner (from the periphery to the center of marrow spaces).
    • Development stages:
      • Near bony trabeculae: myeloblast, promyelocyte, myelocyte.
      • Center of marrow spaces: myelocyte, metamyelocyte, mature granulocytes.
      • 1- Myeloblast: medium to large-sized cell, round or oval nucleus, basophilic cytoplasm.
      • 2- Promyelocyte: clear round nucleus with a prominent small nucleolus.
      • 3- Myelocyte: oval and eccentric nucleus.
      • 4- Metamyelocyte: kidney-shaped nucleus.
