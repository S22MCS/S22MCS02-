  • c) Olfactory cells:
    • Under light microscopy, olfactory cells are interspersed among supporting cells.
    • The olfactory cell is the main sensory neuron of the olfactory organ and consists of:
      • Cell body (cytone or perikaryon): about 7 microns in diameter, with a clear nucleolated nucleus located in the upper part of the epithelium.
      • Two processes:
        • Superficial dendritic process: The olfactory rod.
        • Ends in an olfactory vesicle bearing 5 to 8 olfactory cilia (approx. 2 microns long).
        • These motile cilia are the true receptor organelles, capable of spatial orientation for better signal detection. Biochemically, they are rich in lipids.
        • Deep axonal process: The olfactory filament, which passes through the glassy membrane and enters the chorion.
        • In the chorion, olfactory filaments are surrounded by myelin and Schwann sheaths, grouped into bundles, cross the cribriform plate of the ethmoid bone, and reach the olfactory bulb and lobe.
    • Under electron microscopy, two structural components of the olfactory cell are notable:
      • The olfactory rod: the segment where the cytoplasm is relatively abundant, containing a developed endoplasmic reticulum and mitochondria.
      • In contrast, the cytoplasm in the cell body and olfactory filament is sparse and organelle-poor.
      • The olfactory vesicle: an apical specialization of the olfactory cell with a plasma membrane covered by olfactory cilia of typical fine structure.
